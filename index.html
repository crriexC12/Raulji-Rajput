Raulji Rajput – History, Gotra, Region & Culture

A proud Darbar Rajput community rooted in Gujarat’s warrior traditions

🛡️ Origin and Lineage

The Raulji (or Raolji) surname belongs to the Darbar Rajput community of Gujarat. They belong to the Kshatriya (warrior) caste and trace their lineage to the Chalukya (Solanki) dynastyOf the Agnivansh.

Genealogical records connect Rauljis to the Chalukya branch, affirming their proud Rajput martial ancestry.

📜 Gotra and Kuldevi

  • Gotra: Bhardwaj — descended from sage Bhardwaja, widely adopted by Rajput clans.
  • Kuldevi (Clan Goddess): Kshemkalyani Mata

The temple of Kshemkalyani Mata in Sojitra, Anand district is a sacred site for Raulji families. Worship and vows to this Kuldevi are integral to their cultural identity.

📍 Geographic Distribution

Raulji Rajputs are predominantly found in:

  • Anand district
  • Kheda district
  • Panchmahal district (Godhra)
  • vadodara district

    They are a recognized Darbar caste group with historical roots and contemporary presence in these central Gujarat regions.

🎭 Cultural and Community Identity

The Raulji Rajputs strongly uphold Rajput values of valor, honor, and adherence to ancestral traditions. Kuldevi worship is practiced regularly, especially during family ceremonies, weddings, and festivals like Navratri.

The community is socially active and respected for their leadership roles and commitment to preserving Rajput customs.

🧑‍🎓 Notable Personalities

  • C.K. Raulji – Veteran MLA from Godhra, a key political leader in Gujarat since 1990, associated with the Darbar Rajput community.
  • Dr. Jaideepsinh Raulji – Academic and AI researcher from Vadodara, contributing to computer science education.

    • 🏰 Historical Legacy of the Raulji Clan

    The honorific title “Raulji” traces its origin to the ancient royal epithet “Rawal.” ⚔️ This title reflected valor, nobility, and the proud lineage of the Solanki Rajput dynasty of Gujarat.

    Centuries ago, in the region of Godhra, there ruled a valiant monarch — King Gajsingh. His reign ended in Samvat 245 (around 1189 AD), after which his son, Prince Kahanadevji I, ascended the throne in Samvat 246.

    King Kahanadevji had two queens. The chief queen bore a son named Vachankdevji, and the younger queen, Vandevi, gave birth to Chachankdevji. After the King’s demise in Samvat 297, the throne was bestowed upon Chachankdevji — a decision that stirred rightful discontent in the elder prince.

    Demonstrating wisdom beyond rivalry, the two brothers chose peace over conflict. Vachankdevji received dominion over 325 villages near Anand and Vidyanagar, establishing his royal seat at Bakrol. He also governed the region of Mahida, which became renowned as his ilaka (estate).

    In honor of royal heritage, Chachankdevji adopted the esteemed title “Rawal”, while Vachankdevji’s descendants respectfully embraced the variation “Rawalji.” Over generations, this evolved into the modern and revered surname “Raulji.”

    Today, families bearing the names Raulji and Mahida proudly uphold the legacy of their Solanki Rajput (Darbar) ancestry. Their heritage remains deeply rooted across Kheda, Anand, Panchmahal, Vadodara, Ahmedabad, and surrounding regions of Gujarat — carrying forward a timeless tradition of courage, unity, and honor.
